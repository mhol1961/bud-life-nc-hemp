## Key Findings

### 1. THCA (Tetrahydrocannabinolic Acid)

**What is THCA?**
- THCA is a natural, non-psychoactive compound found in raw cannabis flower
- It is the direct precursor to delta-9 THC, which is known for its psychoactive effects
- When THCA flower is heated (e.g., smoked, vaped, baked), a process called decarboxylation occurs, transforming THCA into THC

**FAQ: Does THCA flower get you high?**
> THCA does not cause psychoactive effects in its raw, unheated state. However, when THCA flower is smoked, vaped, or baked, heat (decarboxylation) converts THCA into delta-9 THC, the compound responsible for the cannabis high. So, consuming THCA flower in these ways will produce a traditional cannabis effect.

### 2. Hemp vs. THCA Flower

**FAQ: Is THCA flower the same as hemp flower?**
> No. Although both come from the cannabis plant, THCA flower is bred for high levels of THCA and low delta-9 THC, offering potent effects after heating. Hemp flower is typically bred for higher CBD and very low levels of both THCA and THC, and usually does not produce notable psychoactive effects. THCA flower is more closely related to marijuana in effect, while hemp flower is known for its mildness or non-intoxicating properties.

**Key Differences:**
- **THCA Flower:** Bred for high THCA content, produces potent psychoactive effects when heated
- **Hemp Flower:** Bred for high CBD content, very low THCA/THC, mild or non-intoxicating properties

### 3. Legality

**Federal Legal Status:**
- THCA flower is federally legal under the 2018 Farm Bill, provided it contains less than 0.3% delta-9 THC by dry weight
- This federal compliance allows for nationwide shipping (with state exceptions)

**FAQ: What does "federally compliant THCA flower" mean?**
> This means the flower has less than 0.3% delta-9 THC by dry weight, as required by federal law. Products meeting this standard are legal at the federal level, but lab testing and clear labeling are essential for verifying compliance. Always review lab certificates of analysis to confirm cannabinoid content.

**FAQ: Is it legal to ship THCA flower?**
> Under federal law, THCA flower can be shipped across state lines if it contains less than 0.3% delta-9 THC by dry weight, making it compliant with the 2018 Farm Bill. However, state laws can vary, some states restrict or ban products based on 'total THC' (including potential conversion from THCA), so always check local regulations before purchasing or shipping.

**State Restrictions:**
Some states have enacted stricter 'total THC' laws that account for potential THCA conversion. Customers are advised to check local regulations before purchasing or consuming.

### 4. Effects

**Raw State Effects:**
- In its raw, unheated state, THCA does not produce psychoactive effects

**After Heating (Decarboxylation):**
- When THCA flower is exposed to heat through methods like smoking, vaping, or baking, the THCA converts to delta-9 THC
- This results in a traditional cannabis high and associated psychoactive effects

**Usage Methods:**
- Smoking
- Vaporizing  
- Infusing into edibles (after heating/decarboxylation)
